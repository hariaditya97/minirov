# ADR-003: Tethered ROV over untethered AUV

## Status
Accepted

## Context
The vehicle could be designed as either a tethered ROV (operator connected via physical cable) or an untethered AUV (fully autonomous, onboard mission execution). Both are viable for UK coastal and lake survey work.

## Decision
Build a tethered ROV for v1. The tether carries both data (ethernet via Fathom-X) and provides a physical recovery mechanism. An untethered AUV is the target for a future iteration.

## Consequences
- Physical recovery guaranteed — tether can always be used to retrieve the vehicle
- Continuous high-bandwidth communication — camera stream, telemetry, and LLM commands over ethernet rather than acoustic modem
- Unlimited endurance potential — tether power upgrade eliminates battery constraint
- Operator always in the loop — supervised autonomy is inherently safer for a first build
- Operational range limited to tether length (50m)
- Tether management adds operational complexity in current and surf
- Cannot execute missions beyond tether range
